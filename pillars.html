Cancer Coach USA framework

The Five Pillars

A calm structure for the practical, emotional, and informational work families face during cancer care.

Inspiration Symptom Care Nutrition Research Resources

How the pillars help

Support organized around the questions that keep coming back.

The pillars are not a medical protocol. They are a coaching map for staying oriented, prepared, and connected while your care team guides clinical decisions.

01

Inspiration

Cancer can make every day feel narrow. This pillar focuses on hope that is honest: meaningful routines, family connection, spiritual or personal grounding, and small wins that make the next step feel possible.

02

Symptom Care

Track changes in speech, mobility, cognition, seizures, sleep, appetite, pain, mood, and medication side effects so appointments can become more specific and productive.

03

Nutrition

Support practical food planning around energy, taste changes, nausea, swallowing concerns, steroids, treatment schedules, and care-team nutrition guidance.

04

Research

Turn complex information into clearer questions about diagnosis, treatment options, second opinions, clinical trials, scans, terminology, and what to ask next.

05

Resources

Gather the practical supports families often need: appointment tools, transportation ideas, caregiver planning, benefits questions, community support, and reliable education.

Start with one pillar

Choose the area that feels most urgent today.

Cancer Coach USA can help organize immediate needs without losing sight of the whole person and the people supporting them.
